# Governance & Taste Protection

## Emotional Truth Lock

> Intimate in the verse, opening up in the chorus.  _(lean: intimate)_

- vocal should remain close and human
- arrangement can bloom but should not become triumphant too early
- the bridge can narrow rather than explode
- the final chorus should release but still carry ache

## Governed Variant Selection

- **Vocal could feel more believable and present.** → winner `vocal_A` (truth 88, taste emotion/craft/identity 85/84/88)

## Listener Panel

- **first time fan**: The chorus feels earned.
- **producer**: Arrangement reads clearly; watch foreground crowding.
- **mixer**: 0 critical masking conflict(s); low-mid/foreground sharing to resolve.
- **artist**: Make sure the bridge/chorus still serves: 'Intimate in the verse, opening up in the chorus.'.
- **playlist listener**: Hook/chorus should land within the first 30s and feel distinct from the verse.

## Stop Conditions

- Should stop iterating: **False**
  - not yet: dynamic movement working
  - not yet: translation safe

- **technical**: Vocal belief: Ride the lead vocal phrase-by-phrase before adding compression.

## Kill-Switches (never auto-applied)

- Never overwrite original audio.
- Never destructively tune or time-stretch source recordings.
- Never delete tracks without backup.
- Never flatten comped vocals without a duplicate.
- Never apply creative source edits without a versioned duplicate.
- Never chase reference loudness at the mix stage.
- Never widen the full mix to solve chorus lift.
- Never make the lead vocal less intelligible unless explicitly approved.
- Never allow a stock loop to dominate the song identity by accident.
