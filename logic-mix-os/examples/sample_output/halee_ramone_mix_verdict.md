## Top Recommended Moves

1. **Vocal belief** — Ride the lead vocal phrase-by-phrase before adding compression.
2. **Section contrast** — Chorus 1 do not lift enough. Create lift through supporting elements and automation (bloom on entry, intimacy in verses), not master level.
3. **Stereo loop / width control** — Splice Texture Loop appear full-width/foregrounded. Narrow to ~35-50%, filter, or push to the background so they are felt, not heard.
4. **Depth cleanup** — Too many elements occupy the foreground. Move supporting guitars/keys/pads to the midground instead of EQ-ing everything.
5. **Low-end definition** — Kick and bass share sub energy. Carve complementary space or sidechain the bass subtly to the kick.

## Production vs. Mix Boundaries

- **'verse_1' feels crowded** → `arrangement_problem`. 3 wide, forward elements share the stereo image in section 'verse_1'. The mix may feel crowded even if levels balance. _Best fix:_ Mute or push decorative/wide elements to the background rather than EQ-ing all of them.
- **Chorus 1 does not lift but is already dense** → `arrangement_problem`. Density increases without an increase in space/width; adding more will not create lift. _Best fix:_ Create contrast by subtraction before the section, then bloom space at entry.

## Negative Constraints (what this must NOT become)

- Do not let the chorus become a wall of sound.
- Do not let stock loops dominate the song identity.
